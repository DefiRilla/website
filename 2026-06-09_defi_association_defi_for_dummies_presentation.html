Method #1
Staking
Lock tokens, earn rewards. A savings account — but the interest, and the value of your capital, might go to zero.
~3–20% APY

How it works

  • Deposit tokens into a staking protocol or validator (a node that processes and confirms transactions)
  • Network pays you rewards for helping validate transactions
  • Rewards are usually in the same token you staked
✅ Simple  ·  ✅ Keep price upside  ·  ✅ Fully passive

Main risks

  • 📉 Token price crashes — rewards are paid in the same token you staked, so 20% APY means nothing if the token drops 90%
  • ⛓️ Lock-up periods — funds are frozen for days or weeks, so you watch the price collapse with no way out
  • 🐛 Smart contract bugs — if the protocol is exploited, funds can be drained instantly with no recourse or insurance

📡 Monitor

Watch the token price. High APY means nothing if the token halves.

🪙 Staked tokens with great APY. Token went to zero. Rewards were worth $0.47. I could have bought four Freddos and had change. Instead I held to zero.

Method #2
Liquid Staking
Stake ETH, get a tradeable receipt (e.g. stETH) you can still use elsewhere. Earn while you earn.
~3–6% + composability

How it works - stETH example:

  • Deposit ETH into Lido/Rocket Pool → get stETH
  • stETH earns staking rewards automatically
  • stETH can also be used as collateral (assets locked as security for a loan) or in other pools — stack yield
✅ No lock-up  ·  ✅ Stack yield on yield  ·  ✅ No 32 ETH minimum

Main risks

  • 💔 De-peg: stETH can trade below ETH during a panic — you can redeem 1:1 if you wait days to unstake, but liquidators won't wait, so your collateral is worth less than you think
  • 🏚️ Collateral collapse: if you borrowed against stETH and it de-pegs, your health factor drops instantly and you get liquidated
  • 🎪 Centralisation risk: Lido controls ~30% of all staked ETH — a hack or regulatory action could impact the whole ecosystem at once

📡 Monitor

Watch the stETH/ETH peg — critical if you've borrowed against it.

💧 Used stETH as collateral to borrow ETH to buy more stETH. "Risk free!" stETH de-pegged slightly. Health factor (your collateral safety score — below 1.0 = liquidated) dropped faster than my self-esteem.

Method #3
Lending & Borrowing
Lend crypto to earn interest — or borrow against yours without selling it. You're the bank now.
Supply 1–15% · Borrow 2–20%

How it works

  • Supply: deposit USDC/ETH → earn interest automatically
  • Borrow: deposit collateral → borrow up to your limit
  • Your Health Factor (collateral value ÷ debt — must stay above 1.0) — hit 1.0 and you get liquidated
🚨 LIQUIDATION = your collateral is seized automatically
Price drops → health hits 1.0 → protocol takes your collateral. Instantly.

Main risks

  • 😴 Silent liquidation: health factor can hit 1.0 while you sleep — collateral seized automatically, no warning, no grace period
  • 🎢 Variable borrow rate: rates can jump from 3% to 30%+ overnight — what was profitable yesterday bleeds money by morning
  • 🐛 Smart contract exploit: Aave, Compound, and others have had close calls — a bug can drain all supplied funds instantly

📡 Monitor (CRITICAL)

Health factor can go from safe to 1.0 while you sleep. Real-time alerts are essential.

Method #4
Liquidity Providing (LP)
Put two tokens into a DEX (decentralised exchange — no company, just code) pool. Every trade through it pays you a fee. You become the exchange.
~5–50%+ fee APR

How it works

  • Deposit equal value of 2 tokens (e.g. ETH + USDC)
  • Traders swap through your pool — every swap pays you a cut of traders fees
  • Withdraw anytime, no lock-up
Used on: Uniswap V2, SushiSwap, and most DEXes

Main risks

  • 🫠 Impermanent Loss (IL): if ETH pumps 2× while you're in an ETH/USDC pool, the AMM (pool's pricing algorithm) sells your ETH as it rises — you end up with less ETH than if you'd just held it
  • 🧮 Fees ≠ profit: you can earn 50% APR in fees and still withdraw less than you deposited once IL is factored in
  • 🏃 Rug pulls & exploits: anonymous devs can drain the pool and vanish — your position goes to zero with no recourse

📡 Monitor

Track real APR vs IL. Many "profitable" LPs are net negative once you do the maths.

🌊 LPd an ETH/altcoin pool for the "amazing" APR. Altcoin did a 10×. My "impermanent" loss was extremely permanent. The fees covered about 3 lattes.

Method #5
Concentrated LP
LP within a price range for way more fees — but if price leaves the range, you earn absolutely nothing.
Up to 100× efficiency

How it works

  • Set a price range (e.g. ETH $2,000–$2,500)
  • Price IN range → massive amplified fees
  • Price OUT of range → zero fees + 100% in worst token
Used on: Uniswap V3, Ambient, Maverick, Izumi

Main risks

  • 💀 Dead position: price leaves your range → zero fees AND the AMM rebalances you 100% into whichever token is performing worse
  • 🔄 Tight range = constant maintenance: higher fees when in range, but any market move kicks you out — forcing rebalancing and gas spend repeatedly
  • ⛽ Gas costs: each repositioning costs real money — on mainnet Ethereum $20–$100+ per adjustment, which can easily erase weeks of fees

📡 Monitor (ESPECIALLY IMPORTANT)

You need an alert the moment you go out of range. Dead positions cost you silently.

🎯 Set a tight range. Left range in 6 hours. Forgot for 3 weeks. Zero fees. 100% in the losing token. Perfectly concentrated. Just not in the way I wanted.

Method #6
Yield Farming
LP in a pool AND earn bonus reward tokens on top. Very high APRs. Usually a red flag, not a feature.
5% → "10,000% APR" 🚩

How it works

  • Provide liquidity → get LP tokens (receipts proving your share of the pool)
  • Stake LP tokens in a "farm" → earn extra reward tokens
  • Autocompounders (Beefy) reinvest rewards automatically
Used on: Curve, Convex, Beefy, Pendle, and hundreds of farms

Main risks

  • 🖨️ Reward inflation: the protocol prints its own token as yield — more issued daily = price tanks — 10,000% APR on a token worth $0 is $0
  • 🏃 Rug pull: anonymous team drains all liquidity in one transaction and disappears — funds are unrecoverable and it happens in seconds
  • 🎲 Double loss: your LP loses to impermanent loss AND the reward token crashes simultaneously — both legs of the trade lose at once

📡 Monitor

Track the reward token price. That "1,000% APR" assumes the reward token holds value. Spoiler: it won't.

🌾 Farmed a token at 4,000% APR. Harvested 50,000 tokens/week. Token went from $0.001 to $0.0000003. I had technically farmed a lot. Financially, I had performed artisanal poverty.

Method #7
Looping
Borrow → buy more → deposit → borrow again. Amplify a yield spread — and everything that can go wrong.
Amplified yield (+ loss)

How it works

  • Deposit stETH → borrow ETH → swap to stETH → deposit again
  • Repeat until you've built a 3–5× leveraged position (leverage = borrowed money amplifying your exposure — gains and losses both multiply)
  • If stETH yields 4% and borrow costs 2% → the spread gets amplified
Used with: DeFi Saver, Instadapp, or manually on Aave/Morpho

Main risks

  • 🎡 Cascading liquidation: at 4× leverage, a 10% price drop hits like a 40% loss — small moves can wipe your entire position instantly
  • 💔 De-peg risk: if stETH trades below ETH, your collateral value falls while your debt stays the same — health factor collapses fast
  • 🩸 Negative carry: if borrow rate spikes above stETH yield, the spread that made the loop profitable disappears — you now pay to hold the position

📡 Monitor (CRITICAL)

Leverage multiplies health factor sensitivity. A 5% move can liquidate a 5× loop.

🔄 Looped stETH 4×. stETH de-pegged slightly. Health factor critical before I'd finished my morning coffee. Unwound at a loss. The loss was also 4× amplified. Leverage works both ways. Apparently.

Method #8
Leveraged Yield Farming
Borrow extra capital to make a bigger LP position. More fees — also more IL, also liquidation risk. Fun!
High risk / High reward

How it works

  • You have $1,000 of your own. You deposit it as collateral, then borrow $2,000 more from the protocol
  • You deploy the full $3,000 into an LP position — earning fees on 3× the capital you actually own
  • If fees cover the borrow cost: great. But IL and losses are also 3× — it cuts both ways
Used on: Alpha Homora, Gearbox Protocol

Main risks

  • 📢 Amplified IL: borrowed capital means impermanent loss scales with your leverage — a 3× position has 3× the IL exposure of a normal LP
  • 🤖 Auto-liquidation: protocol seizes your LP position automatically when debt ratio exceeds the limit — usually at the worst possible market moment
  • 💸 Negative carry: borrow rates can spike above LP fee income overnight — you're silently losing money while appearing to be productive

📡 Monitor

Track debt ratio and net APR (fees minus borrow cost minus IL) in real time.

⚡ Explained this to my mum. She said "sounds like borrowing from one bank to put in another bank that might be on fire." She wasn't wrong. I did it anyway. The bank was on fire.

Method #9
Stablecoin Yield
Earn yield on USD-pegged tokens. Lower volatility — not zero risk. "Stable" is doing a lot of heavy lifting here.
~4–25% APY

How it works

  • Lend USDC/USDT/DAI on Aave → earn supply interest
  • LP stablecoin pairs on Curve → earn fees with minimal IL
  • RWA (Real World Asset) protocols (Maker, Ondo) → earn T-bill (US treasury bond) yields on-chain
✅ No price volatility  ·  ✅ Great "parking" yield  ·  ✅ Easy to compound

Main risks

  • 💔 De-peg: USDC hit $0.87 during the SVB collapse — your "stable" $10,000 became $8,700 overnight. UST hit $0 in 2022, wiping out billions
  • 💣 Protocol exploits: Curve lost $70M in 2023 via a compiler bug — smart contract risk exists even in battle-tested protocols
  • 🏛️ Centralised control: Circle (USDC) and Tether (USDT) can blacklist any wallet — your funds can be frozen without warning or recourse

📡 Monitor

Track the stablecoin peg in real time — especially if used as collateral.

💵 Put $5,000 USDC in a vault before the SVB bank run. USDC hit $0.87. Not technically a loss — just a very educational weekend and a new appreciation for anxiety.

Method #10
Perps & Leverage Trading
Perpetual contracts — no-expiry price bets using up to 50× leverage. Unlimited upside. Also unlimited downside. Usually just downside.
Unlimited both ways 💀

How it works

  • Open a leveraged long (bet price rises) or short (bet price falls) on crypto — GMX, Hyperliquid, dYdX
  • 10× leverage = 10% move against you → fully liquidated
  • Pay/receive a funding rate (recurring fee keeping the perp price anchored to the real asset) to hold the position
✅ Hedge other positions  ·  ✅ Earn funding rate  ·  ✅ Profit without a price bet

Main risks

  • ⚡ Instant liquidation: 10× leverage means a 10% adverse move wipes your entire margin — position closed automatically, no warning, no appeal
  • 🦈 Liquidation hunting: market makers know where liquidations cluster — price often spikes just enough to trigger them, then immediately recovers
  • 🔄 Funding rate flip: what started as earning a rate can flip to paying one — your "passive" trade becomes a cash-burning position overnight

📡 Monitor

Watch your liquidation price at all times. A leveraged position left alone is a gift to the liquidators.

📈 Opened a 10× long. Went to make dinner. ETH dropped 11% in 40 minutes. Came back to cold pasta and no ETH. The pasta survived. The portfolio did not.

Bonus Method
Airdrop Farming
Use new protocols early, get free tokens when they launch. The word "free" is doing a lot of work here.
Free tokens (maybe)

How it works

  • Use a new protocol early → earn "points" or usage history
  • Protocol launches token → distributes to early users
  • Famous examples: Uniswap $UNI, Arbitrum $ARB, ENS
✅ Earn on top of existing strategies  ·  ✅ Some worth thousands

Main risks

  • 📉 Launch dump: early farmers, VCs, and insiders all unlock at TGE (token launch day) and sell simultaneously — token price can drop 80–90% within hours of launch
  • 🚫 Sybil filter: protocols flag "suspicious" wallets and exclude them from the drop — months of effort, zero reward, no appeal process
  • ⏰ Opportunity cost: your funds earn nothing for months while the protocol may delay, change terms, or never launch a token at all

📡 Monitor

Get instant alerts when tokens land — first movers sell at launch price, not 3 days later.

🪂 Farmed a protocol 6 months. Token launched at $2.00. By morning: $0.12. Earned $47 for 200 hours of work. That's $0.235/hour. McDonald's was hiring.
